# HelpyCords v2.0 - FL Studio Guide

HelpyCords is designed to help you play like a professional by automatically correcting your notes to match the selected chord and key.

## How to use in FL Studio for "Pro" Recording

To record the corrected notes (the "pro" notes) rather than the keys you physically press:

1.  **Add HelpyCords to your project**:
    - Open the Plugin Database and drag `HelpyCords` into your Channel Rack.
2.  **Enable Auto-Correct**:
    - Make sure the **AUTO-CORRECT** button in the HelpyCords UI is turned ON.
3.  **Setup MIDI Output**:
    - HelpyCords is configured to output MIDI data.
    - If you want to record the corrected notes into FL Studio's Piano Roll:
        - Right-click the Record button in FL Studio and ensure "Notes" is selected.
        - When you record, FL Studio will capture the MIDI output from HelpyCords.
4.  **Practice Mode**:
    - Turn OFF **AUTO-CORRECT** to practice your timing.
    - The keys will still highlight in **Gray** (chord tones) and **Green/Red** (your performance) to help you learn, but it will play exactly what you hit.

## Controls
- **Instruments**: Choose between Piano, Guitar, Saxophone, Violin, Harp, and more.
- **Chord/Key**: Select the scale you want to play in.
- **Effects**:
    - **Pitch**: Transpose your playing (+/- 1 octave).
    - **Speed**: Adjust the envelope speed.
    - **Tone**: Change the brightness of the instrument.
    - **Delay/Reverb**: Add space to your sound.
    - **Compression**: Smooth out the volume.
- **Piano Keyboard**:
    - Shows a 24-key chromatic view.
    - **Slide Octaves**: Use your **Mouse Wheel** over the keyboard to move between C0 and C8.
